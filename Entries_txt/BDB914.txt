Biblical Hebrew

בָּדָד
noun [masculine]
isolation, separation: Isa 27:10
עִיר בְּצוּרָה בָּדָד the fenced city is isolation, i.e. is solitary
(substantive for adjective : Dr^§ 189. 2^); more usually as adverb
accusative, to signify alone, Deut 32:12 especially with verbs of dwelling, Lev 13:46
בָּדָד יֵשֵׁב he shall dwell alone (literally in isolation
), Jer
15:17; Lam 1:1
; Lam 3:28:
figurative of freedom from attack, security Deut 33:28 (of Israel), Jer 49:31 (Kedar); soלְבָדָד

Mic 7:14; Ps 4:9 for thou
לְבָדָד לָבֶטַח תּוֺשִׁיבֵנִי makest me dwell solitarily, in safety (see Deut 33:28).
