Hab 3:13. — Of
Psalms with סֶלָה, 23 used in Elohistic Psalter, 28 in Director's Psalter, 39 in final editing of
Psalter. These editors found it in earlier Psalters. Davidic Psalter uses 20 of them, so few in proportion that it
is not characteristic of this Psalter; but Korahite 9 (out of 12), and Asaph 7 +

Hab 80:8 [

ᵐ5

] (probably 8 out of 12); apparently it came into use in time of these editors. In Diretor's Psalter
musical terms are added to 19 of the 28 it uses (and only to ten others, of which some could hardly use סלה
); Psalms with סלה all (except 61, 81) name the kind of Psalm in title: 3 מִכְתָּם
, 7 מַשְׂכִּיל, 10 שִׁיר, 26 (27) others מִזְמוֺר, Hab 3
תְּפִלָּה; ׳
ס is especially frequent with שִׁיר and מַשְׂכִּיל, terms
associated with musical rendering. It probably came into use in late Persian period in connection with Psalms used
with musical accompaniment in public worship, to indicate place of benedictions. It was not added by later editors
to other psalms; but was revived in first century B.C., and continued in use for some time (see Jacob^ZAW xvi (1896), 129 f.^
